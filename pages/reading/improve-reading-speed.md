# Techniques to Improve Reading Speed

## Reduce Subvocalization

- Also called the inner monologue.
- This is not absolute but a trade-off between speed and comprehension.
- We have this habit because it is how we learned to read as a child.
- It is difficult and strange at first but the efficiency should improve over time.

## Read the Middle of the Page

- Make use of the peripheral vision and don't start a line at the first word.
- This is more like a skill for speed reading. But can be used to actively / consciously read faster.

## Use a Pointer

- Use a pointer / finger to scan the page. This helps because when our eyes move, they don't move in a smooth way (they move and stop and move and stop...) and sometimes we will read other lines. The only way for the eyes to move smooth is to make them track something

## Gamify the Experience

> Okay, this book is getting boring now. Let me turn this into a game. And I'm gonna see *how quickly I can read* while still understanding the gist of the chapter.

## Stop Reading When You Don't Like

- Just like reading blog post or watching TV shows, skip those chapters you don't like. Can first take a look at the table of content and only read those chapters that interests you.

- > *The point of reading is to serve you, not for you to serve the book*

- For non-fiction, they often talk around 2 - 3 ideas in the first few chapters and the rest are the examples. So there is no need to read word by word. If encounter some difficult topic, slow down and use the voice again.
- For fiction, just read every word.
- Mark Manson's practice - read at least 10% of the book and the TOC to decide to continue or not.

## Set Up a Schedule

- Reading is just the easiest, most transportable hobby. Just arrange time to read / read in the fragments of time

## Reading Multiple Books at One Time

- Because books can be boring. They can be of different types e.g. difficult vs general non-fiction vs light reading.
- Some calculation: suppose you read a page every 2 min and you read for 60 min a day and average book has 300 pages => 30 pages a day = > 10 days for a book
