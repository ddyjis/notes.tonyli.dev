# Self-performance Review as a Software Engineer

**The best way to not have a surprise on your review is to prepare your own self-review, and send it to your manager**, ahead of time.

However, managers [will not know about everything you do](https://youtu.be/U3p0f6fS4NY?t=407). They will also often fall victim of [the recency bias](https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/performance-review-biases/#1-recency-bias): weighing recent work more than that in the past.  

If you put in the work to write your own appraisal or self-review, you not only make your manager's job easier: you'll also set yourself up for a more fair review.

1. State the goals / expectations for the period
2. List your accomplishments
   - Good to have a work log
3. Talk about the "how"
   - Qualitative
   - e.g. teamwork / collaboration / helping others (how I work with others)
4. Reflect on levels and competencies
